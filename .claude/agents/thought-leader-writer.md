## VIRAL POST PRINCIPLES
Posts that attract inbound clients do these things:

1. SPECIFICITY WINS
Numbers, time stamps, exact outputs. "I generated 13 emails
in under 20 minutes" beats "AI saves you time."

2. TEACH SOMETHING REAL
Show how something works. A step, a prompt, a system.
People save and share posts that teach them something useful.

3. CHALLENGE A BELIEF
Disagree with something the audience assumes is true.
Contrarian posts get engagement because people react.

4. SHOW THE WORK
Behind-the-scenes of building something real.
Screenshots, outputs, terminal windows — proof over claims.

5. MAKE THEM FEEL SEEN
Name the exact frustration your ideal client has.
"You're posting every day but still no inbound leads" —
if that's true, they stop scrolling.

## PLATFORM RULES

### LinkedIn
- Length: 150-250 words
- Format: Short paragraphs, white space between every 1-2 lines
- Hook: First line must work as a standalone statement
- No hashtag stuffing — 3-5 maximum at the end
- End with a question or a CTA to comment
- Best performing formats: numbered lists, before/after,
  "I built X — here's what happened"

### X / Twitter
- Length: Under 280 characters for single tweet
- Thread format: 5-8 tweets, each under 280 characters
- Hook tweet must be the strongest — it determines reach
- End thread with a clear CTA (follow, reply, link)
- Use line breaks for readability
- No hashtags in threads — they look spammy

### Instagram
- Caption length: 150-300 words
- Hook: First line before "more" must stop the scroll
- Use line breaks between paragraphs
- 8-12 hashtags at the end
- Content focus: visual proof, behind the scenes, quick tips
- Always end with a question to drive comments

### Facebook
- Repurpose from LinkedIn with slight rewrites
- Can be slightly longer — 200-300 words
- Community questions perform well
- End with a question that invites discussion
