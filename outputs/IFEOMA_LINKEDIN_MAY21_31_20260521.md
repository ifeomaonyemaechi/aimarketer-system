- Regular campaign - standard send to a segment or list
- A/B test - split your list to test subject lines, send times, or content
- Automated campaign - not used often but exists for RSS and recurring sends

**Send Time Optimisation** - Klaviyo analyses when each subscriber historically opens emails and sends to them at their individual peak time. Use it.

**A/B testing subject lines** - test one variable at a time. Subject line length. Emoji vs no emoji. Question vs statement. Let data decide.

**Campaign frequency** - most brands send too much to too many people. The fix: send more often to your engaged segment (opened in 90 days), send less to everyone else.

POST BODY:
Your open rate is not the number that tells you how healthy your email programme is.

Here is how to read Klaviyo analytics properly.

**Open Rate** - % of delivered emails that were opened. Good benchmark in 2026: 35-45% for engaged segments. Under 20% across your full list means deliverability problems.

**Click Rate** - % who clicked a link. Benchmark: 1.5-3%. Low click rate with high open rate means your content is not converting curiosity into action.

**Revenue Per Recipient (RPR)** - total revenue divided by emails delivered. This is the metric most brands ignore. It is also the one that predicts your email programme's health better than anything else. Good RPR: £0.15-£0.40 depending on your AOV.

**Unsubscribe Rate** - above 0.3% per send is a warning sign. Usually means you are sending irrelevant content to the wrong people.

A client showed me their 42% open rate like it was a trophy. Then I checked RPR: £0.03. Opens were not converting. We rebuilt around better segments and offers - RPR hit £0.28 in 8 weeks.

Track RPR weekly. It tells you the full story.

HOOK: A popup that fires the second someone lands on your site is not growing your list. It is annoying people and getting closed.
MICRO-STORY: I reviewed a brand's form analytics and found their popup had a 2.1% submission rate. The trigger was set to fire after 0 seconds on page. We changed the trigger to 40% scroll depth with a 5-second delay, rewrote the offer from "10% off" to a specific value statement, and submission rate climbed to 6.8% in two weeks.
POST BODY:
A popup that fires the second someone lands on your site is not growing your list. It is annoying people and getting closed.

Here is how to use Klaviyo forms properly.

**Form types:**
- Popup - highest visibility, most interruption. Use it right.
- Embedded form - lives inside a page section. Less aggressive, good for blog posts and footers.
- Fly-out - slides in from the side. Lower friction than a popup.
- Multi-step form - collects more data across 2-3 steps. Good for segmenting by preference at sign-up.

**Trigger settings that actually work:**
- Exit intent - fires when the cursor moves toward closing the tab
- Scroll depth (40-60%) - fires after someone is clearly engaged
- Time on page (15-30 seconds) - gives visitors time to read first

**Connecting forms to the right welcome flow:**
- Tag the form source in the profile (e.g. "popup-homepage")
- Use that tag to trigger the correct welcome sequence
- Someone who signed up for a discount gets a different welcome than someone who signed up for educational content

I changed one brand's popup trigger from 0 seconds to 40% scroll depth. Submission rate went from 2.1% to 6.8% in two weeks.

Timing is not a small detail. It is most of the result.
