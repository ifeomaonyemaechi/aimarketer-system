Email clients - Gmail, Apple Mail, Outlook - are not websites. They render within a constrained window. 600px wide is the safe zone for desktop rendering across all major clients.

Go wider and your layout breaks on Outlook. Go narrower and you waste screen space.

But desktop is only part of the equation.

71% of email opens happen on mobile. A 600px email that is not built responsively will stack incorrectly, crop images, and render CTAs as thin slivers on a 390px phone screen.

Here is my design process for every client email:

1. Design at 600px in Figma - full desktop layout
2. Define the mobile breakpoint behavior - what stacks, what hides, what resizes
3. Build in Klaviyo using the desktop design as the spec
4. Test on 8 email clients using Litmus before sending

I inherited a Klaviyo account once that had all emails designed at 800px. Mobile open rate was 71% of their list. Their click rate was 0.8%.

One width change was not the full fix - but it was the first one.
