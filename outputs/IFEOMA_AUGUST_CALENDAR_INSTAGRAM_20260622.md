POST BODY:
600px. That number matters more to your email click rate than your subject line.

Let me explain.

600px is the standard email width. It renders correctly on desktop, scales down properly on mobile, and does not get clipped by Gmail.

Go wider and you risk:
- Text becoming unreadable when scaled on iPhone
- Buttons too small to tap
- Images cropped in preview

Mobile-first email design is not optional anymore. Over 60% of emails are opened on mobile.

Here is the checklist I use before any email goes live:

- Width: 600px maximum
- Body copy: minimum 16px, preferably 18-22px
- CTA button: minimum 44px height, full width on mobile
- Line height: 1.5 minimum for readability
- Single column layout on mobile (always)

I design every email in Figma at 600px before touching Klaviyo.

That step catches every readability and hierarchy problem before it reaches a subscriber.

Icon: optional but effective. A simple arrow or chevron increases click intent.
